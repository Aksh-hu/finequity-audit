RBI Regulatory Submission

Regulatory Submission Package

This platform enables community members to submit formal complaints to RBI under the Digital Lending Directions 2025 and RBI's Fair Lending Framework. Your documented evidence becomes part of regulatory investigations.

Complaint Title

Systematic Algorithmic Discrimination in Microfinance Lending: Evidence from Participatory Community Audit of 3,156 Loan Decisions

Formal Complaint

We formally submit evidence of systematic algorithmic discrimination in microfinance lending affecting 1,247 borrowers across 45 Self-Help Groups in Tamil Nadu. This discrimination violates RBI's Non-Discriminatory Lending Framework and Digital Lending Directions 2025.

Key Allegations

  1. Gender Discrimination: Women borrowers face 28 percentage point lower approval rates than men with identical income, collateral, and repayment history. This represents systematic exclusion of 99% of microfinance users.
  2. Caste-Based Discrimination (via Occupational Proxy): Handloom workers and domestic workers (95%+ SC/ST) face 41-50 percentage point lower approval rates than tech sector workers (5% SC/ST). The algorithm uses occupation as a proxy for caste.
  3. Geographic Discrimination: Rural borrowers face 14 percentage point lower approval rates than urban borrowers with identical profiles. This systematically excludes 80% of microfinance users who live in rural areas.
  4. Interest Rate Extraction: Marginalized borrowers are charged 4-8 percentage points higher interest rates, extracting ₹2.3 crores annually from 1,247 borrowers documented in this audit.
  5. Processing Time Discrimination: Women face 9-day longer processing times; rural borrowers face 13-day delays—causing seasonal loans to miss planting windows and emergency loans to arrive late.

Regulatory Basis

These allegations violate:

  • RBI Master Direction on Fair Lending (Section 4.2 - Non-Discriminatory Lending)
  • Digital Lending Directions 2025 (Section 5 - Borrower Protection and Transparency)
  • Constitution of India Article 15 (Prohibition of Discrimination)
  • Microfinance Institutions Network (MFIN) Code of Conduct

Requested RBI Action

  1. Formal investigation of lending algorithms at Major NBFC A, Major NBFC B, Bank Partner, Local MFI, and other entities identified
  2. Mandatory algorithmic transparency: lenders must disclose decision logic to RBI
  3. Mandatory recalibration of algorithms to remove identified bias factors
  4. Audit trail requirements for all lending decisions with demographic tracking
  5. Regulatory penalties for non-compliance with Non-Discriminatory Lending Framework
  6. Mandatory interest rate equalization where discrimination proven
  7. Borrower notification rights when demographic factors influence decisions

Recommendations for RBI Action

1. Immediate Investigation (Within 30 Days)
  1. Formal inquiry into lending algorithms of identified entities
  2. Request algorithmic documentation and validation protocols
  3. Audit of loan decision data for demographic patterns
  4. Preliminary findings report within 60 days
2. Regulatory Requirements (Within 90 Days)
  1. Algorithm Transparency: Lenders must disclose to RBI how algorithms weight each factor (gender, location, occupation, etc.)
  2. Demographic Data Tracking: All lenders must track and report approval rates, interest rates, loan sizes by gender, geography, occupation, caste proxy
  3. Fair Lending Testing: Mandatory quarterly "paired testing" with identical applicants varying only demographics
  4. Audit Trail Requirements: Complete decision logs showing which factors influenced each decision
3. Algorithmic Remediation (Within 120 Days)
  1. Removal of identified bias factors from algorithms
  2. Recalibration to achieve demographic parity in approval rates
  3. Interest rate equalization across demographic groups with same risk profile
  4. Independent validation by third party before redeployment
4. Borrower Protections
  1. Mandatory borrower notification when demographic factors affect decisions
  2. Structured appeal rights with regulatory oversight
  3. Protection against retaliation for filing bias complaints
  4. Right to human review if algorithmic decision impacts >₹50,000
5. Community Participation
  1. Legitimize participatory algorithm auditing as regulatory tool
  2. Create formal grievance mechanism for community-documented bias
  3. Mandatory lender response to community audit findings
  4. Public reporting of lender compliance with fairness requirements
6. Enforcement & Penalties
  1. Significant penalties for non-compliance (₹1-5 crore depending on scale)
  2. Mandatory remediation of wrongful rejections (re-offer loans at fair terms)
  3. Interest rate rollback and refund of discriminatory charges
  4. License suspension for repeat offenders
